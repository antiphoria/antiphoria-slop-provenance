### Part 1: General Hardwired Smoke Detector Wiring Steps

Most hardwired smoke detectors in residential settings (in North America) connect to 120V AC power and often have a battery backup. They typically come with a wiring harness (pigtail) that plugs into the back of the detector.

**Tools You Might Need:**
*   Non-contact voltage tester
*   Screwdriver (Phillips and/or flathead)
*   Wire strippers
*   Wire nuts (usually supplied with the detector or included in the junction box)
*   Lineman's pliers (optional, for twisting wires)
*   Ladder

**Typical Wires You'll See (coming from your ceiling junction box):**
*   **Black:** Hot/Live (120V)
*   **White:** Neutral
*   **Green or Bare Copper:** Ground
*   **Red or Orange (Optional but Common):** Interconnect wire (for communicating with other hardwired smoke detectors so they all alarm together)

**Typical Wires on the Smoke Detector's Pigtail Harness:**
*   **Black:** Connects to house Hot
*   **White:** Connects to house Neutral
*   **Green:** Connects to house Ground
*   **Red (Optional):** Connects to house Interconnect

**Wiring Procedure:**

1.  **TURN OFF POWER AT THE BREAKER.** Verify power is off with your voltage tester.
2.  **Remove Old Detector (if applicable):** Disconnect the old detector from its wiring harness and mounting bracket.
3.  **Prepare Wires:**
    *   Ensure the wires from the ceiling junction box are stripped to the correct length (usually about 1/2 to 3/4 inch, check your detector's manual).
    *   Take the new smoke detector's wiring harness.
4.  **Connect Wires (Match Colors):**
    *   **Ground:** Twist the **green** wire from the smoke detector harness together with the **green** (or bare copper) ground wire from the ceiling box. Secure with a wire nut.
    *   **Neutral:** Twist the **white** wire from the smoke detector harness together with the **white** neutral wire from the ceiling box. Secure with a wire nut.
    *   **Hot:** Twist the **black** wire from the smoke detector harness together with the **black** hot wire from the ceiling box. Secure with a wire nut.
    *   **Interconnect (IF APPLICABLE):** If you have multiple hardwired smoke detectors that you want to interconnect, twist the **red** wire from the smoke detector harness together with the **red** (or orange) interconnect wire from the ceiling box. Secure with a wire nut. If you only have one detector or no interconnect wire, cap the red wire from the harness with a wire nut and ensure any red wire from the ceiling box is also capped off and not used.
5.  **Tuck Wires:** Carefully push the connected wires and wire nuts back into the junction box.
6.  **Attach Mounting Bracket:** Screw the new smoke detector's mounting bracket securely to the junction box.
7.  **Connect Harness to Detector:** Plug the wiring harness into the back of the smoke detector.
8.  **Mount Detector:** Align the detector with the mounting bracket and twist it clockwise until it locks into place (check manual for specific locking mechanism).
9.  **Install Battery Backup:** Open the battery compartment (if applicable) and install the recommended batteries (e.g., 9V or AA/AAA). Close the compartment.

### Part 2: Performing a Smoke Test

Once your smoke detector is wired and mounted, it's crucial to test it. There are generally two types of smoke tests:

**A. Button Test (Standard & Recommended)**

This tests the alarm circuitry and power connection.

1.  **Restore Power:** Go back to your electrical panel and turn the circuit breaker back ON.
2.  **Initial Chirp/Light:** Many detectors will give a quick chirp and/or flash their LED light when power is restored, indicating they're powered up and performing a self-check.
3.  **Press and Hold Test Button:** Locate the "Test" button on the front or side of the smoke detector. Press and hold it for a few seconds.
4.  **Listen for Alarm:** The alarm should sound loudly. If it's interconnected, all other connected smoke detectors should also sound their alarms within a few seconds.
5.  **Release Button:** The alarm should stop once you release the button.
6.  **Verify LED:** The LED light (if present) should usually flash periodically (e.g., once every 30-60 seconds) to indicate it's receiving power and functioning correctly.

**B. Simulated Smoke Test (More Thorough, Optional for Homeowners)**

This tests the actual smoke sensing chamber. Only do this if you have a product specifically designed for it, or if recommended by the manufacturer.

1.  **Purchase Canned Smoke (Aerosol Smoke Detector Tester):** These products (e.g., "Smoke Detector Tester" spray) are designed to safely produce a non-toxic, harmless synthetic smoke. **DO NOT use real smoke (e.g., from a candle or burning paper), as it can leave residue, damage the sensor, or trigger a false fire alarm.**
2.  **Apply Smoke:** Follow the instructions on the canned smoke product. Typically, you'll spray a short burst (1-2 seconds) from about 1-2 feet away directly into the vents of the smoke detector.
3.  **Listen for Alarm:** The alarm should sound within a few seconds. If interconnected, all other detectors should also sound.
4.  **Clear Area:** Once tested, ventilate the area to clear the simulated smoke and allow the alarm to silence.
5.  **Battery Only Test:** If your detector has a battery backup, you can also test it by temporarily turning off the power at the breaker (while the detector is still wired) and then performing the button test. This confirms the battery backup is working.

**Important Considerations & Maintenance:**

*   **Test Regularly:** Smoke detectors should be tested monthly using the button test.
*   **Replace Batteries:** Replace battery backups at least once a year (e.g., when you change clocks for daylight saving time).
*   **Clean Detectors:** Dust and debris can interfere with smoke detectors. Gently vacuum around the vents with a soft brush attachment annually.
*   **Replace Detectors:** Smoke detectors have a lifespan. Most manufacturers recommend replacing them every 7-10 years, even if they appear to be working. There's often a "replace by" date printed on the back.
